The world this week<h1>Business</h1><p>5月 21, 2026 04:01 上午</p><img src="/images/articles/TE-2026-05-23-00004.jpg" title="Elon Musk."/>
<p><b><a href="../../feed_4/article_0/index_u40.html">SpaceX·</a></b> officially launched its blockbuster <span>IPO</span>. The space-rocket firm, led by Elon Musk, hopes to raise around $75bn when it floats on the<span> NASDAQ</span> stockmarket, smashing the record for an <span>IPO</span> held by Saudi Aramco, which went public in 2019. Its target valuation is about $1.75trn. In its prospectus SpaceX laid out ambitions to use its rockets to transport components to off-world data centres in space. It also mentions a vision of carrying people to Mars and of mining on asteroids.  The <span>IPO</span> announcement came as the firm prepared the first test flight of its <span>V</span>3 Starship megarocket.</p>
<p>It was a mixed week for <b>Elon Musk</b>, who lost his courtroom battle with Open<span>AI</span> and Sam Altman. It took a jury just two hours to reject the case, in which Mr Musk sought damages from Open<span>AI</span> for allegedly reneging on a contract with him as a co-founder to run the firm as a non-profit instead of for-profit entity. Meanwhile, Open<span>AI</span> was reportedly ready to launch its own <span>IPO</span> with a view to a listing in September. https://t.me/demagazinesharing</p>
<p><b>Nvidia</b> presented another stellar set of quarterly earnings. It recorded overall revenues of $81.6bn, up by 85% year on year, with sales from chips for data centres taking in $75.2bn, a rise of 92%. The company announced an additional $80bn in share buy-backs.</p>
<p>A last-minute deal between management and unions averted a strike at <b>Samsung</b>. Samsung has reaped huge profits from selling its memory chips to data centres and the unions want their cut in  bonuses. </p>
<p><b>Britain’s</b> annual inflation rate fell to 2.8% in April from 3.3% in March, mostly a result of the government lowering its cap on energy prices. Electricity prices fell by 8.4%. However the cost of motor fuel surged by 23%, prompting the government to postpone a rise in fuel tax this week. Economists expect the overall inflation rate to rise again in the coming months.</p><h4>When in need</h4>
<p>America extended a waiver on sanctions for buying <b>Russian oil</b> for another 30 days. The waiver applies to Russian oil held at sea and is designed to help poorer countries that have taken a hit from the stoppage of supplies through the Strait of Hormuz. Meanwhile, Britain eased sanctions on Russian oil refined into diesel and jet fuel in other countries. This will enable the country to import jet fuel from India.</p>
<p><b>Ryanair</b>, Europe’s largest airline, said it was “far too early” to give any meaningful profit guidance because of volatile jet-fuel prices. Europe is relatively well stocked with jet fuel, and Ryanair hedges 80% of its fuel costs, but it warned that spot prices had hit $150 a barrel for unhedged fuel.</p>
<p>Adani Enterprises, part of <b>Adani Group</b>, agreed to pay $275m to settle claims that it violated American sanctions by buying Iranian fuel between November 2023 and June 2025. The case was brought by America’s Treasury Department. Meanwhile, the Justice Department decided to drop its criminal fraud case against Gautam Adani, Adani Group’s chairman, and the Securities and Exchange Commission settled with Mr Adani on allegations that focused on investors being misled about his group’s anti-bribery practices. Under the deal Mr Adani neither denies nor admits the claims. </p>
<p><b>Anglo American</b> agreed to sell its coalmining assets in Australia for up to $3.9bn to <b>Dhilmar</b>, a privately held mining firm. Anglo has now divested its coal, nickel and platinum divisions and is in talks with a consortium to sell off its De Beers diamond business.</p>
<p>A formerly unsolved <b>mathematical problem</b> has been cracked by a model from Open<span>AI</span>, in what has been called a “milestone” in <span>AI</span> maths. The problem, which asks how many pairs of points can be exactly one unit apart on a two-dimensional plane, was first posed in 1946 by Paul Erdos. The <span>AI</span> proof overturned previous best efforts, and was produced by a general model without any mathematics-specific training, said Open<span>AI</span>.</p><h4>How to make a quick buck</h4>
<p><b>Swatch’s </b>Royal Pop pocket watch went on sale. Made in collaboration with Audemars Piguet, the watches combine the retail and luxury ends of the market. Hundreds of people queued outside Swatch shops around the world; some disturbances were reported as customers fought over a timepiece. Selling for $400-420, some watches are being resold online for as much as $3,000.</p>
<p><b>James Murdoch</b>, son of Rupert and a former boss of News Corporation in Europe, is acquiring a roughly 50% stake in <b>Vox Media</b>. Mr Murdoch wants to be involved in “longer-form, thoughtful journalism”, a contrast to the cut-and-thrust of the daily newspapers he used to run. One of the titles owned by Vox is <i>New York</i> magazine, which publishes articles on Gotham as well as stories of national interest, such as why “The Amish Are Falling in Love With <span>AI</span>”.</p>
