3:T1990,<p><span><span><span>The world this week</span></span></span></p><p><span><span>The world this week</span></span></p><p><span><span>Dec 30, 2025 05:49 AM</span></span></p><p><img src="/images/articles/TE-2026-01-03-00089.jpg"/></p><p>Volodymyr Zelensky met Donald Trump in Florida for talks. Both men said progress had been made towards resolving issues to end the <a href="https://www.economist.com/topics/ukraine-at-war"><span>Ukraine war</span></a> (after their meeting Mr Zelensky suggested he wanted a security guarantee to last up to 50 years). Mr Trump acknowledged that the “thorny” question remained of Russian territorial claims. Meanwhile, Russia attacked Ukraine with 500 drones and 40 missiles, leaving 1m homes in and around Kyiv without power.</p><p>After a <a href="index_split_037.html#filepos197175">meeting in Florida with Binyamin Netanyahu·</a>, <span>Israel’s</span> prime minister, Mr Trump said he wanted to push forward with the peace deal for Gaza and urged Hamas to disarm. He also said he would support another attack on <span>Iran</span> if it resumed its weapons-building programme.</p><p>A few days earlier <a href="index_split_038.html#filepos202165">Israel became the first country to recognise ·<span>Somaliland</span></a> as an independent, sovereign state. Both Israel and Somaliland said their joint declaration of mutual recognition was in the spirit of the Abraham accords, American-brokered peace deals between Israel and other countries in the region. Somalia described it as an “unlawful step” that threatened its sovereignty. China issued a statement saying it firmly opposed any attempt to split Somalia.</p><p>The main separatist group in southern <a href="index_split_036.html#filepos188546"><span>Yemen·</span></a>, the Southern Transitional Council (<span>STC</span>), rejected a call by <span>Saudi Arabia</span> to pull back from areas it had captured in early December and which has pushed the Saudi-supported Yemeni government out of its base in Aden. The <span>STC</span>, which is backed by the United Arab Emirates, said that Saudi Arabia had conducted air strikes against its forces (which the Saudis did not acknowledge). </p><p>At least three people were killed in <span>Syria</span> as clashes broke out at several demonstrations held by the Alawite community. The protests came shortly after a bomb at an Alawite mosque in the city of Homs killed at least eight people. In March 2025 hundreds of Alawites died amid an outbreak of sectarian violence stirred up by supporters of the ousted Assad regime.</p><p>The Nigerian government confirmed that America’s recent missile strikes against terrorists in northern <span>Nigeria</span> had targeted two camps with links to Islamic State. President Bola Tinubu supported the action to stop militants from the Sahel infiltrating the area, the government said.</p><p>Mr Trump said that America had carried out an attack on a “dock area” in <a href="https://www.economist.com/interactive/the-americas/2025/11/05/tracking-american-drug-boat-strikes-off-venezuela-coast"><span>Venezuela</span></a> used by drug gangs, the first known direct strike on the country by the <span>US</span>. Reports suggested that the attack was a drone strike conducted by the <span>CIA</span> on a port facility used to store drugs. No one was hurt, according to the reports. The Venezuelan government didn’t comment.</p><p>Marco Rubio, America’s secretary of state, congratulated Nasry Asfura on winning the presidential election in <span>Honduras</span>. The election was held on November 30th but the result was delayed amid technical problems and claims of voter fraud. Mr Asfura is backed by Mr Trump. Mr Rubio urged Honduras’s opposition parties to respect the outcome.</p><p><a href="https://www.economist.com/topics/argentina"><span>Argentina</span></a><span>’s </span>Congress passed a budget, the first since Javier Milei became president in December 2023. Since taking office Mr Milei had merely extended the 2023 budget.</p><p><span>China</span> carried out huge military exercises in the seas surrounding <span>Taiwan</span>, which it said were intended as a “serious warning to Taiwan independence separatist forces and external interference forces”. The drills, which encircled the island, used live rounds. Taiwan recently secured an $11bn deal with America for weapons, the largest package of its kind, and has greatly increased its defence budget.</p><p><img src="/images/articles/TE-2026-01-03-00062.jpg"/></p><p><span>Cambodia</span> and <span>Thailand</span> agreed to halt the fighting that had broken out for a second time in recent months along their disputed border. After the ceasefire was announced both sides went to China for talks with the country’s foreign minister, Wang Yi.</p><p><span>America’s economy</span> grew at an annualised rate of 4.3% in the third quarter, according to a first estimate that had been delayed by the government shutdown. It was the <a href="index_split_064.html#filepos354571">fastest pace of growth in two years·</a> and in part reflected an increase in consumer spending, allaying fears about the economic impact of tariffs.</p><p>Alaa Abd el-Fattah, an Egyptian activist who was given British citizenship in 2021, moved to <span>Britain</span> three months after his release from an Egyptian prison. Sir Keir Starmer, the prime minister, and other senior ministers said they were “delighted”, but that was before a series of tweets surfaced from 2010-12 in which Mr Abd el-Fattah expressed support for killing Zionists and the British police, and that he hated white people (he has apologised amid the furore). With ministers denying knowledge of the tweets, the Foreign Office started an investigation into “serious information failures”.</p><p>Earlier, the British government increased the threshold at which a new tax kicks in on <span>inherited farm land</span> from £1m ($1.35m) to £2.5m. Farmers’ associations, which have been protesting against the controversial tax, broadly welcomed the decision, but noted there would still be a large tax bill for some.</p><p>Shortly before Christmas America’s Department of the Interior suspended leases for five <span>wind farms</span> off the east coast. The government cited “national security risks” and floated the possibility that the turbine towers interfere with radar.
